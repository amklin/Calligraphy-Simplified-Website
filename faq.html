Canvas Instructions

To use the canvas, click on settings in the upper right corner of the screen.

Each of the buttons that pops up toggles a different setting.

refresh: Clears the canvas. You can also use the escape key to clear the canvas.

visibility: Turns the canvas on and off without erasing what is currently on the canvas.

line_weight: Use the slider below the icon to adjust the line width.

color_lens: Use the color picker below the icon to change the color.

The canvas default is off. To turn on the canvas for the first time use the refresh button.

Warning: Don't change the window size while writing. If you change the window size, use the refresh button to ensure that everything functions correctly.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • layersWhat is the difference between Mandarin and Cantonese?
    Mandarin and Cantonese are two of the most commonly used dialects within the umbrella of Chinese languages. Although they use the same writing systems, speech is not mutually intelligible. Other dialects include Shanghainese, Fuzhounese, and Sichuanese.
  • filter_vintageIs Chinese a language?
    Technically, it is not; Chinese is an umbrella term for the different dialects.
  • interestsWhat is the difference between simplified and traditional, and which one should I learn?
    Simplified and traditional are two different Chinese writing systems. Simplified, as the name implies, has simpler characters. It was created in the 1950s in an effort to improve literacy rates. Currently, simplified is mostly used in Mainland China, while traditional is mostly used in Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan. This website teaches simplified Chinese because it is considered easier to learn, as well as being used by more people. What you personally should learn depends on what you want to use Chinese for.
  • local_libraryWhat is pinyin, and why is it important?
    Pinyin is the Romanization system for Mandarin Chinese. It uses Latin letters and four diacritics to represent the four tones (ā, á, ǎ, à). Groups of letters correspond to the pronunciation of one Chinese character.

    Pinyin is used to teach Mandarin, as well as a pronunciation guide. Additionally, many Chinese input systems use pinyin. For example, when typing Chinese characters on a computer or phone, chances are that you will be using pinyin.
  • languageWhy learn Mandarin Chinese?
    Mandarin Chinese is the most commonly spoken first language in the world. By learning it, you open up many avenues for communication.
  • visibilityIs Chinese difficult to learn?
    While it may be difficult at times, learning Chinese, just like learning any other second language, requires only hard work and perseverance.
  • ruleWhat are tones and why do they matter?
    Chinese is a tonal language, meaning that each word has a different tone that can affect meaning. For example, 马 (mǎ) means horse while 妈 (mā) means mom. If you’re not careful, you might end up accidentally calling your mom a horse!
